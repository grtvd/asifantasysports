12. Trades

Trades will only be available from May 4th, 1999 until August 31, 1999. Once an Owner has proposed a trade, the Owner receiving the proposal has at least 48 hours to either accept or decline the trade. If no action is taken the trade will expire and will be considered declined.

The Owner proposing the trade may clear the trade if the trade has not yet been accepted or declined. Once a trade has been accepted or declined, it cannot be cleared.

Once the Owner receiving a trade takes action on the trade, either accepts or declines that trade, that action becomes final.

After a trade has been accepted, other Owners in the league have at least 48 hours to review the trade. If they feel that the trade affects the competitive balance of the league, they can protest it.

If greater than 33% of the Owners not involved in the trade, protest it, the trade will be disallowed. Owners may only protest 2 trades per season.

Trades will be processed in the order they where proposed. A completed trade may invalidate a pending trade if any of the same players are involved.

Trades take precedence over free agent claims. A completed trade will clear a free agent claim if the same player is involved. A free agent claim will not be processed if the player being released is involved in a trade.

When submitting a trade, WWFB will require that players are selected for release in order to maintain a 28 player roster. If an Owner's roster has fewer than 28 players, WWFB will still require the selection players for release as if the roster had 28 players. When the trade is actually processed, WWFB will only release the number of players required to fulfill the 28-player limit. Players will be released in the order selected.

FAIR PLAY: Two Owners completing a trade that have obvious benefits towards only one of the Owners is collusion and is prohibited. Any Owner found in violation of this will be disqualified from the Contest and access to all teams will be revoked.
