6. The Draft

Many considered the draft to be the most important part of fantasy game playing. Teams with a strong draft tend to do well throughout the season. Therefore, Owners should prepare for the draft by researching what players they feel will perform the best in the upcoming NFL season.

Most on-line fantasy games use a system where they draft one position at a time. In real life, teams are allowed to draft any player at any time they want regardless of position. Since "live" drafts are difficult to complete in a Web environment, WWFF's draft takes place off-line, and thus, relies on a Owner's single draft ranking. This ranking can include any active player from the NFL at the following positions (Quarterback, Wide Receiver, Running Back, Tight End, Kicker and Defensive Team). If you feel Barry Sanders, a running back, is more valuable than Troy Aikman, a quarterback, then you would rank Sanders higher, regardless of their positions.

Each Owner will draft 14 players consisting of players from the following positions:
2 Quarterbacks
3 Wide Receivers
3 Running Backs
2 Tight Ends
2 Kickers
2 Defensive Teams
Since it can be very time consuming to rank a few hundred players, as well as very difficult, WWFF does a few things to make it easier.

First, each Owner is provided with a default draft ranking. This draft ranking ranks the players based on the total points of their previous year's statistics. Be aware that injured players or rookies would be ranked very low (or not ranked) but could be very valuable. This draft ranking can be modified to suit each Owner's desire. The default draft ranking contains 168 players. This is to allow for a maximum of 12 teams to draft 14 players. If your league contains fewer teams, then fewer players would need to be ranked. A league with 10 teams would only need 140 players to be ranked.

Second, WWFF breaks the draft into 4 days, multiple rounds happening each day. For each of the first three days, 4 rounds will take place with each Owner drafting 4 players. For the last day, 2 rounds will take place with each Owner drafting 2 players.

Breaking the Draft into multiple days allows Owners to analyze the players chosen so far, to review the remaining available players, and to adjust their rankings as necessary. After each day, any players drafted are removed from the draft rankings. Be sure to review your ranking after each day and adjust as needed.

The letters (A through J) are merely symbolic representations of the teams in a league and are used to show relative draft orders across multiple draft rounds. Round 11 would be the same as Round 1, Round 12 would be the same as Round 2, and so on.

When it is an Owner's turn to pick a player, the Owner's ranking is scanned for the highest ranked player who meets the following two conditions: 1) the player has not already been picked, 2) the Owner has open slots at the player's position. For example, if the Owner has already drafted 2 quarterbacks, then any remaining quarterbacks would be skipped when looking for the next player to draft.

This will result in a balanced lineup to start the season.
